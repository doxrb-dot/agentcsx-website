Is Agentcsx Safe? How We Protect Your Account & Your Data

It's a fair question — and one we'd want answered before installing anything that touches our LinkedIn account. So let's answer it plainly. No vague "your security is important to us" filler. Here is exactly how Agentcsx works, what it can and can't see, and where your data lives.

There are really two kinds of "safe" people mean when they ask about a tool like this:

  • Account safety — will this get my LinkedIn restricted or banned?
  • Data safety — what happens to my data, and who can see it?

We'll cover both.

1. It runs in your own LinkedIn session — the same way you do

Agentcsx is a Chrome extension that works inside your own logged-in browser. It acts through your existing LinkedIn session — the same one you're already signed into — not from some remote server pretending to be you.

That has two important consequences:

  • We never ask for your LinkedIn password. There's no LinkedIn login to hand over, because the extension simply uses the session you've already created in your browser.
  • There's no risky third-party server logging into your account. Many "cloud" automation tools log into LinkedIn from their own data centers — a pattern LinkedIn actively flags. Agentcsx doesn't do that. Activity comes from your real browser, on your real connection.

2. Human-paced automation that respects LinkedIn's limits

The fastest way to get an account restricted is to blast hundreds of connection requests in minutes. Agentcsx is built to avoid exactly that.

  • Daily & weekly caps. Connection requests are capped well within LinkedIn's safe limits, and new accounts ramp up gradually instead of going full-speed on day one.
  • Randomized, human-like pacing. Requests are spaced out with natural delays rather than fired off in a burst.
  • You stay in control. You can review and approve actions, or let things run hands-off within those safe limits.

The goal is simple: grow your network like a diligent human would — not like a bot.

3. Local-first: your data stays on your device by default

By default, your leads, campaigns, and settings are stored locally in your browser using Chrome's built-in storage. That information never leaves your device unless you choose to turn on cloud sync.

If you never sign in, you can use Agentcsx as a purely local tool — nothing about your pipeline is sent to us.

4. Optional cloud sync — and it's locked down

If you want a backup (so you don't lose your leads when you switch computers), you can sign in with Google and enable cloud sync. When you do:

  • Google sign-in shares only your email and name, purely to identify your account. We never see your Google password.
  • Your data is encrypted in transit and at rest. Everything moves over HTTPS/TLS and is stored encrypted in our database (Supabase).
  • Row-Level Security (RLS) isolates every account. At the database level, each user can only ever read or write their own rows. Your leads are walled off from every other user — it's enforced by the database, not just the app.

5. We don't sell or share your data. Period.

This one is short because it should be: we do not sell, rent, trade, or share your personal information or your leads with third parties. Your data exists for one reason — to power the features you use inside the extension.

6. AI that scores leads without exposing your data

Agentcsx uses AI (Google Gemini) to score leads and help draft messages. A few things worth knowing:

  • The AI runs through a secure server-side proxy. Our API keys live on the server, never in your browser, so they can't be scraped or abused.
  • No personal data is stored by the AI service. Prompts are used to generate a result and aren't retained as a profile of you.

7. Minimal permissions, each with a clear purpose

An extension can only request the permissions listed in its manifest — and we keep ours lean and scoped to LinkedIn. Here are the key permissions and why they exist:

  • cookies — to use your existing LinkedIn session (so you don't re-enter credentials).
  • storage — to save your leads and settings on your device.
  • alarms — to schedule periodic status checks (e.g. "did this lead accept?").
  • tabs — to open LinkedIn profiles and handle sign-in.
  • linkedin.com access — to interact with LinkedIn's interface on your behalf.

Notably, Agentcsx does not request access to your other websites, your browsing history, or your saved passwords.

8. You're in control of your data

Trust means you can leave whenever you want, with everything you came with:

  • Export your leads to CSV anytime.
  • Delete all local data instantly by uninstalling the extension.
  • Request deletion of any cloud-synced data by emailing us.
  • Opt out of the cloud entirely by simply not signing in.
🔒 The short version

Agentcsx runs in your own browser session, paces itself to keep your account safe, keeps your data on your device by default, encrypts and isolates it if you opt into cloud backup, and never sells it. You can export or delete everything at any time.
